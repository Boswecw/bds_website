Core principles

Public architectural language should be plain enough to trust and specific enough to matter.

Intent clarity

Start by naming what the system must actually do, not by collecting fashionable components and hoping they form a whole.

Structure

Products should reflect the underlying sequence of work so operators can understand the system instead of memorizing exceptions.

Recoverability

Failures should degrade in understandable ways, preserve authority, and leave the operator with a clear next move.
